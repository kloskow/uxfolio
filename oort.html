OORT

This project has been published in VRST '23: Proceedings of the 29th ACM Symposium on Virtual Reality Software and Technology

Project Abstract

The project aims to improve the long-term wearing comfort of head mounted displays by reducing a skin temperature of the facial areas covered by the headset.

The integrated cooling system consists of an electronically controlled fan blower. The cooling compartment is integrated into a HMD padding element with custom-designed air flow channels that provide cool air circulation around the covered facial regions.

User Study

Our research aimed to asses the integration of the active padding component and measure thermal comfort of the participants throughout the VR experience. In addition, we analyzed the air circulation inside the HMD padding. We measured changes of temperature on the skin surface, under the HMD padding and the HMD microclimate with and without OORT
